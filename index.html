We met in college...

at a social function at NIU. The timing was off.
A few years later, after we both had graduated,
we saw each other again at a Greek new member induction ceremony.
Peter was one of the pledge dads.
We started talking about career stuff since he was about to graduate
(I know how to rope ‘em in good, don’t I? 😉 )
Through my aggressive flirting techniques,
I pressured him to go on a doggie play date with our pups, Pogi and Bijou.
The rest as they say, was history. ~Susan

He Proposed

May 2015

Woodstock, New York

  • HER VERSION

    He organized a weekend upstate to celebrate our 7th anniversary and rented an adorable guesthouse through Airbnb. It was owned by a lovely older British couple and the property was surrounded by lush acreage and hanging wisteria. The morning after we arrived, we stopped to get brunch and coffee and then set out to for a hike up a local mountain - his photog equipment in hand and all. After reaching the top, he searched for two perfect trees to set-up a two-seater camp-style hammock.

    He paced back and forth looking for the right spot. Then he decided not to set it up just yet and said, “Let’s take some pictures by the cliff.” Okay, sure... After a few photos, he was at it again. Pacing back and forth amongst the trees. After what felt like 10 minutes (it probably wasn’t even close), I impatiently said, “Here. Why not here?!” He obliged and set it up.

    I sat inside the two-seater hammock waiting for him to finish draping the mosquito net and tarp. Before you know it, he recklessly climbed in and the first thing I said was, "You are SO sweaty!" I know, sweet of me right? Not even a thank you for setting up this comfy hammock that I have been relaxing in all by myself that I now have to share with this sweaty person (eyeroll). He started talking about the adventures we had together and asked me what my favorite one was. This was weird. He does not do small talk. But sure, I’ll go along with it. I explained to him that my favorite experience was a snowboarding trip we took a few years back in Whistler - because HE planned it and our more recent trip to Hawaii. Then he asked, "So we're going to have more adventures together, right?" I said, "Of course, we already talked about that." (Long, silent pause) I turned my eyes towards him and realized his eyes were welled up.

    Me: "Wait, are you proposing right now?"

    Him: "Yeah."

    Me: "Ohhhh."

    Him: "What's the matter?"

    Me: "Uh...it's just not how I imagined it was going to happen."

    Him: "How did you think it was going to happen?:

    Me: "I don't know...maybe you'd get down on one knee and present me with a ring?"

    Him: "Are you disappointed? I thought we were going to look at rings in the fall."

    Backstory: We spoke about taking a trip out to LA in the coming fall to visit our families and visit a jewelry showroom I mentioned that I wanted to go to. He had been making such a big deal about booking our flight for the last few weeks that I didn’t imagine he would propose to me until once we'd looked at rings.

    Me: "I'm not disappointed (lie - yes I was)."

    While I sulked in the hammock, he climbed over me to get out and within seconds I heard some rustling amongst the leaves. I turned to my right. He had gotten down on one knee, and presented me with box and asked, "Will you marry me?" Of course, then I squealed with joy, teared up and screamed, "YESSSS!!!" I hugged him harder than I ever had before. We didn't call our families until the next day and enjoyed being secretly engaged for the rest of the hike. He proposed in true "Peter fashion." I should have known better. Anytime he has ever gotten me anything I've really wanted, he's always created a false sense of disappointment before the big reveal. He proposed with an old world european cut diamond set in a vintage-inspired setting with filigree detail.

Café Brauerclose

Café Brauer is a restaurant building and official landmark located in Chicago, Illinois, at the edge of the South Pond in the Lincoln Park Zoo.
It was designed by Dwight H. Perkins and completed in 1908. The restaurant, originally commissioned by the Brauer family, was one of the most popular eateries in Chicago in the early twentieth century.
The building, known for its green roof, red bricks, and lagoon-side setting, has been called "an outstanding example of the Prairie School of architecture."
Its second-floor Great Hall is the centerpiece, with its soaring, skylit roof, Tiffany-inspired glass chandeliers, stained-glass windows, tile mosaics and flanking, curved outdoor loggias providing spectacular views of the surrounding park and city skyline.
A newly landscaped circular drive greets guests on Stockton Drive, but the horticultural pièce de résistance lies immediately to the east and south: Nature Boardwalk at Lincoln Park Zoo, a thriving prairie pond ecosystem teeming with native plants and wildlife.
